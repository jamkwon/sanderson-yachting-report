TLDR

Sanderson has real traction, a sharp messaging direction, and a founder who knows the category cold. What's missing is the organic search infrastructure to turn that expertise into traffic, and a systemized way to convert that traffic into qualified inquiries. Here are the five things that matter most right now:

Biggest SEO Gap

Sanderson ranks for 21 keywords. Epic Yacht Charters (similar authority) ranks for 1,230. The difference isn't content quality — it's that Sanderson has zero destination landing pages, an underbuilt pricing page, and an all-inclusive page sitting at position 90. Building 12–15 targeted pages against low-difficulty keywords (KD 0–18) could 5–7x organic traffic within 6 months.

Fastest Win

Sanderson already ranks #1 for "how much is a yacht charter" but gets near-zero clicks — the SERP snippet likely needs rewriting. A 10-minute title tag fix + expanding the pricing page into a comprehensive cost guide targets a cluster worth 2,450+ monthly searches at KD 3–7.

Messaging is Dialed

"Say Yes to Luxury Yacht Clients" is the right headline. The three pillars — Give the Most Get the Most, White-Glove White-Labeled, and From Inquiry to Itinerary — give us a complete StoryBrand-aligned framework. Now it needs to get onto the site, into the advisor kit, and into every conversion touchpoint.

Biggest Revenue Lever

The travel advisor channel. The messaging is sharp, the model is differentiated, and the competitive landscape is wide open — Charter Smarter's /travel-agent/ page ranks for nothing. A dedicated /for-travel-advisors/ landing page with the full StoryBrand flow, plus a downloadable Advisor Kit, is the single highest-ROI asset we can build in Month 1.

What We Need From Erin

Messaging approval, pricing details/ranges for the cost guide, confirmation on destination priorities, and access to current advisor-facing materials for refinement. Everything else — the page builds, the SEO optimization, the HubSpot work, the content creation — Figmints owns and executes.

What Each Competitor Actually Did Right (and Where They're Beatable)

Epic Yacht Charters — The Benchmark

Epic is the clearest study in "doing SEO right in this category." At DR 27, they're not a domain authority powerhouse — they just built the right pages. Their homepage alone ranks for 376 keywords and pulls 3,758 visits/month because it targets "luxury yacht charter," "yacht charters," "private yacht charter," and "caribbean yacht charter" simultaneously. Their playbook:

  • Destination-specific landing pages: /yacht-search/bahamas/ ranks for 112 keywords, drives 868 visits. These are just well-structured destination pages with targeted H1s and internal links.
  • Cost/pricing content: /booking/yacht-charter-cost/ ranks for 135 keywords, drives 535 visits. Same content lane as Sanderson's pricing page — Epic just has stronger on-page optimization.
  • "All-inclusive" as a dedicated play: They rank #1 for "all inclusive yacht charter caribbean" and #2 for "all inclusive yacht charter greece." Sanderson's all-inclusive page sits at position 90.
  • Below Deck content: Their crewed charter/Below Deck page drives 279 visits from 138 keywords. Sanderson's Below Deck page gets 10 from 11 keywords. Same topic, wildly different optimization.
The Implication: Epic proves you don't need DR 60+ to win in yacht charter SEO. You need targeted pages with clear keyword focus and reasonable on-page optimization. Sanderson can replicate this playbook within 6 months.

DMA Yachting — Misleading at First Glance

DMA has a DR of 45 but only 5 ranking US keywords and 11 organic US visits. This confirms what Erin suspected: DMA's strength is likely in European/international markets and across multiple supporting microsites. In US organic search, DMA is essentially invisible on their main domain. Don't use DMA as a US SEO benchmark — use Epic.

Charter Smarter — Beatable but Instructive

At DR 12, Charter Smarter is slightly weaker than Sanderson in authority but pulls 442 visits from 134 keywords — 15x Sanderson's traffic from 6x the keywords. Their BVI itinerary page drives 55 visits from "bvi 7 day sailing itinerary." They also have a /travel-agent/ page that ranks for almost nothing — an opening for Sanderson to own that lane decisively.

The Real Organic Gorilla: YachtCharterFleet.com

DR 70, 12,525 keywords, 143,324 monthly visits. This is the category incumbent. Sanderson won't outrank them on head terms like "yacht charter" (KD 73) soon. But YachtCharterFleet is an inventory aggregator — they don't own education, pricing transparency, or "which charter is right for me?" Sanderson wins in the advice layer, not the inventory layer.

9. Domain Authority — How to Grow from DR 14

Sanderson has 63 referring domains. Epic has 232. For Tier 1 keywords (KD 0–20), Sanderson's current authority is often sufficient. Here's the pragmatic path:

High-leverage backlink opportunities:

  • Travel advisor associations and directories: Any association Erin speaks at or partners with should link to Sanderson.
  • Boat show and industry event coverage: 45+ boat shows = 45+ potential link sources from event sites, recap articles, exhibitor directories.
  • PR placements: The outside PR vendor should be securing links, not just mentions. Every placement should include a followed link to a relevant page.
  • Guest content on advisor-facing publications: Erin's "how to sell yacht charters" expertise is genuinely valuable content for these outlets.
  • Yacht listing aggregators: YachtCharterFleet, CharterWorld, etc. — ensure Sanderson is listed with active links.
Realistic DR trajectory: DR 14 → 25–30 within 6–9 months. That puts Sanderson in the same class as Epic and unlocks Tier 2 keywords. The key is 10–15 new referring domains per month from relevant sources.
